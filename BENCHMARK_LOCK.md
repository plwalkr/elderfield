# BENCHMARK_LOCK

BM-01 `Warden's Rise` is locked only when all of the following are true:

- `Readability`: the player can identify the main landmark, safe NPC space, enemy lane, optional reward pocket, and both exits within the first second.
- `Landmark Dominance`: the Warden Stone clearly wins the upper-terrace silhouette and reads as the screen's main promise.
- `Road Rhythm`: the south road pulls the eye cleanly to the stairs, then branches with purpose instead of wandering.
- `Cliff Language`: cliffs read as consistent SNES-style terrain with a clear lip, face, and passable stair break.
- `Tree Massing`: trees function as deliberate route framing and silhouette mass, not decorative scatter.
- `Structure Read`: the house, ruin pocket, and landmark all feel modular, material-specific, and authored from the same visual language.
- `Reward Pocket`: the briar gate and watch-cache alcove feel intentional, memorable, and readable before and after unlock.
- `Prototype Removal`: there are no invisible-feeling blockers, no obviously placeholder silhouettes, and no screen areas that read like generic filler.
- `Systems Stability`: movement, combat, save/load, pause, debug, and progression all still function without bespoke hacks outside the benchmark pipeline.
- `Reusability`: another overworld screen could be rebuilt by following BM-01's tile logic, composition rules, and asset structure without reinventing the style.
